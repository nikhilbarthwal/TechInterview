About

About me:

I currently work at Google and in the past, I have worked for Microsoft and other companies. Over the last decade or so, I have been interviewed by many companies. Therefore, I have considerable experience in how tech companies evaluate interview candidates. I have also been involved in recruitment for the organization I worked for, as a result I have gained experience on both sides of the table.

I get a lot of questions from other people about how to get a job in top-tier tech companies in general: What is the best way to apply, what kind of questions are asked, how to ace the interview etc. So I have created this website in an attempt to answer all these questions for everyone.

The story of this site:

Back when I was doing my PhD, Microsoft came to university for recruitment. I applied for a job as Software Developer and was shortlisted for an interview. To prepare for the interview, I read several books, practiced coding questions on paper and took lot of notes. I aced the interview and started working for Microsoft.

In the next decade or so, I switched a couple of jobs and was interviewed by several companies. I reused the notes I had prepared initially and kept on updating them with more content. At one point of time, I realized that these could be of use to others as well, so I am now making all the information I have public.

I hope that the material provided here would help you ace your tech interview. If you have any comments or suggestions, please feel free to contact me.
