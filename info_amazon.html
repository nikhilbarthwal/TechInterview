In Amazon, candidates are interviewed for specific teams. Amazon interviews use the industry standard format that covers the fundamentals of computer science (Data Structures & Algorithms).

Initially the recruiter will give you information on the team/position, explain the process and help you prepare for the interview. This might (sometimes) be followed by a call from the hiring manager who will tell about the team and specific role he/she is looking to fill. Following that, there will be a phone interview which will have a coding question and sometimes a system design question also for senior candidates. If you clear that, you move to the on-site stage. The on-site stage will consist of 4 to 5 interviews and a separate lunch interview.

One thing particular about Amazon interviews is the concept of “Bar raiser”. The interview panel will have one person called the bar raiser who comes from another team. This person has veto power over the outcome of your interview and exists to make sure every team hires candidates of equal caliber. While the candidate is not told who the bar raiser is, it is easy to spot him/her. Just ask everyone which team they belong to and what they do and you will notice that one person will have a different answer from the rest. The hiring manager and the bar raiser are the two most important people in panel though others are important too. Amazon does blind interviews, so interviewers will not share feedback with each other until they enter it into their system.

Interviews are a mix of coding, behavior and system design. Coding interviews are done on white board where the candidate will write code. The interviewer will examine the logic of the code and ignore minor syntax issues. For system design, the candidate will come up with a design on a whiteboard. For Amazon ( specially AWS), pay special attention to scalability in your system design.

In a behavioral interview, the interviewer will ask questions about certain situations that you faced in the past and focus on how you handled that situation. Amazon has 14 Leadership principles that they want you to know and the interview will be looking for examples of how you apply them in practice. They prefer that you answer in STAR format.

Recruiter will follow-up with you after a few days with a decision. If they reject you, there is a waiting period before you can be interviewed again. If the outcome is positive, Amazon will make you an offer
